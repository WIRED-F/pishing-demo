5 signs this page is a phishing attack
1
Fake / Spoofed URL

3
No HTTPS / Missing Padlock
The URL starts with http:// not https://. Your password travels unencrypted and can be intercepted by anyone.
4
Broken / Fake Legal Links
Terms, Privacy, and Cookie links go nowhere. Phishers clone the look but can't replicate real working pages.
5
Copied Footer as Camouflage
The footer is copy-pasted to appear trustworthy. Regardless of how it looks, the URL is the only source of truth.
The golden rule of phishing protection
Before entering any username or password, look at your browser's address bar. The real domain — the part just before .com / .net / .org — must exactly match the site you intend to visit. If anything looks off, close the tab and navigate directly by typing the address yourself.
